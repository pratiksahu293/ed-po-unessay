Youth of Color Transforming Power Through Digital Media

I argue that youth media engagement is a source of power—not harm. Through digital platforms, young people challenge stereotypes, organize resistance, and build identity outside traditional institutions.

Click Each Card to Explore My Research

The Issue

The Issue

Mainstream media often portrays youth of color through narratives of danger, failure, and risk rather than creativity and leadership. This shapes how society views youth and reinforces harmful racial hierarchies.

My Position

My Position

I argue that digital media strengthens youth voice, identity, and political consciousness. Youth are not passive consumers—they actively reshape media and challenge racism through online participation.

What I Found

What I Found

Youth of color use TikTok, Instagram, and YouTube to mobilize communities, share lived experiences, educate peers, and expose systemic injustice. Digital spaces now function as political classrooms.

Why It Matters

Why It Matters

When youth control media, they reclaim identity, challenge racism, and build collective power. Schools and institutions must recognize youth as creators—not just consumers.
